Crime and Covid

This data story is about how crime is represented within society and how it evolves through a pandemic such as Covid-19.

Visualisation 1: Crime Index for European Countries represented as a Heat Map and Line Chart over time.

This chart shows the crime index for European countries per year from 2014 to 2021.

An interactive slider can be used to change the year being viewed on the geographical heat map.

We can see on the right line chart that the UK is highlighted as it is the focus of this datastory.

We also see how the UK Crime Index changes over the years and can notice it consistently is above the EU average.


Visualisation 2: Bubble Clouds showing Offence Count per Policing Region in Northern Ireland and per Offence Type for 2020/2021.

This chart shows the most dangerous areas within Northern Ireland along with the most commonly commited crimes.


Visualisation 3: Parallel Sets Showing the distribution of offences affecting both Male and Female.

This chart shows us that females are impacted more so than males overall but also noticeably more for crimes such as harassment.


Visualisation 4: Offence Count Change over time in Northern Ireland.

The line chart shows how overall crime has changed over the years, we can clearly see that overall offence count has decreased while the Covid-19 pandemic was affecting Northern Ireland.

The bar chart takes into consideration the total offence count per crime for the 2 years prior to the Covid-19 outbreak, followed by the change seen for the 2 years of the Covid-19 pandemic.

It is staggering to see on a high level overview that crime has only increased by 0.9% however when we look under the hood, crimes such as harassment have increased by nearly 140%!
